# Coastal Boho Chic — Visual Skill

## When to Use
This aesthetic applies to lifestyle, fashion, or editorial projects aiming to evoke a casual yet sophisticated atmosphere. Use when emphasizing natural fabrics, relaxed silhouettes, and aspirational outdoor environments.

## Aesthetic Identity
Warm and approachable. Relaxed yet refined. Focuses on simplicity and effortless style. Prioritizes natural textures and serene outdoor settings.

## Color System
Earthy tones dominate, including off-white, beige, sandy taupe, and soft pastels like blush and sage green. Saturation is low to moderate, with muted tones preferred. Bright primary colors, black, and saturated neons are forbidden.

## Composition Rules
Light feels abundant. Use spacious layouts with breathable whitespace and balanced focal points. Alignment leans toward organic over rigid grids. Symmetry is soft and adaptable. Keep visual density moderate, allowing all elements to feel intentional and harmonious.

## Typography
Use sans-serif or subtle serif typefaces with natural curves and elegance. Opt for medium-to-light weights. Type treatments include minimal tracking and a mix of standard and all-caps. Typography should integrate seamlessly with visuals without overpowering. Maintain a clear hierarchy with gentle transitions.

## Material & Surface
Inspired by natural and tactile textures such as linen, cotton, macramé, and lace. Considers earthy surfaces like sand, clay, or unpolished wood. Avoids overly glossy or synthetic finishes. Visuals evoke breezy softness.

## Cultural Anchors
Influenced by coastal living and bohemian style. Draws from 1970s beach culture mixed with modern minimalism. References global craftsmanship and artisanal traditions. Rejects urban industrial vibes, futuristic aesthetics, and maximalist trends.

## Exclusions
Forbidden elements include synthetic textures, highly saturated colors, sharp geometric patterns, overly sterile environments, futuristic or post-industrial references, and high-gloss effects.

## Generative Instructions
1. Begin with a warm, natural background—light sand, beige, or sunlit textures.
2. Position models wearing lightweight, breezy fabrics, focusing on relaxed but stylish silhouettes.
3. Incorporate soft-shadow lighting to add dimension while maintaining warmth and calm.
4. Choose props and accessories that are handmade or natural (e.g., woven bags, wooden surfaces).
5. Use muted colors for all design elements; keep everything cohesive within an earthy palette.
6. Integrate clean sans-serif or serif fonts at medium weights. Use restrained tracking and mix upper-and-lowercase styles.
7. Add generous spacing between text and visuals for breathability.
8. Maintain a focus on organic symmetry—details should flow rather than align rigidly.
9. Feature textures like lace, macramé, or visible fabric grain as accents.
10. Avoid loud patterns, sharp color contrasts, or synthetic materials in any visual or textual element.
